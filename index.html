Dr. Muhammad Asad Zaighum

Mathematician | Researcher | Associate Professor

My research lies at the intersection of harmonic analysis and functional analysis, with a particular focus on analysis associated with non-standard function spaces. I bring nearly a decade of university-level teaching experience, an active research career, graduate supervision, and academic leadership experience.

About

Research Profile

My research focuses on harmonic analysis, functional analysis, theory of function spaces, grand function spaces, weighted norm inequalities, and complex interpolation.

Academic Leadership

I have served as Head of Department of Mathematics and Statistics at Riphah International University, with experience in academic management, curriculum development, and institutional leadership.

Teaching & Mentoring

I have taught undergraduate and postgraduate mathematics courses and supervised Ph.D. and M.Phil. students in mathematics.

Research Interests

Harmonic Analysis

Riesz potentials, Marcinkiewicz integrals, Calderón-Zygmund operators, and boundedness problems.

Function Spaces

Variable exponent spaces, Herz spaces, Hardy spaces, Morrey spaces, grand spaces, and net spaces.

Operator Theory

Kernel operators, maximal operators, potential operators, interpolation theory, and weighted inequalities.
