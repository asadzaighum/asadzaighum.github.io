Dr. Muhammad Asad Zaighum

Mathematician | Researcher | Associate Professor

My research lies at the intersection of harmonic analysis and functional analysis, with a particular focus on analysis associated with non-standard function spaces. I have nearly a decade of university-level teaching experience, an active research profile, graduate supervision experience, and academic leadership experience as a department chair.

About

Research Profile

My research focuses on harmonic analysis, functional analysis, theory of function spaces, grand function spaces, weighted norm inequalities, and complex interpolation.

Academic Leadership

I have served as Head of Department of Mathematics and Statistics at Riphah International University, gaining experience in academic management, curriculum development, and institutional leadership.

Teaching & Mentoring

I have taught undergraduate and postgraduate mathematics courses and supervised Ph.D. and M.Phil. students in mathematics.

Research Interests

Harmonic Analysis

Riesz potential operators, Marcinkiewicz integrals, Calderón-Zygmund operators, and related boundedness problems.

Function Spaces

Variable exponent spaces, Herz spaces, Hardy spaces, Morrey spaces, grand function spaces, and net spaces.

Operator Theory

Kernel operators, maximal operators, potential operators, interpolation theory, and weighted inequalities.
